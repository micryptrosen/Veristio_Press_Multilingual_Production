## Reviewer Instructions

When a real named native-language reviewer is assigned, review Draft V2 for:

- natural Spanish.
- regional neutrality or the explicitly chosen Spanish locale if one is later selected.
- tone/register for education and workforce readers.
- title treatment, including whether `Human Creativity + AI Synthesis` should remain preserved.
- terminology decisions, including `IA`, `fuente de verdad`, `manuscrito canonico`, `flujo de trabajo gobernado`, `listo para traduccion`, `prompt`, and `trabajo en equipo responsable`.
- awkward literal translation.
- omitted or added meaning against the locked source identity.
- technical or cultural ambiguity that should be escalated to terminology or technical review.

## Reviewer Must Not

- Alter the canonical English source.
- Edit Book 1.
- Treat this review as technical review.
- Treat this review as final sync/proof review.
- Claim Spanish publication readiness.
- Claim release approval.
- Prepare upload-ready files.

## Required Review Record When Performed

The reviewer record must include:

- reviewer name.
- reviewer role.
- review date.
- review status.
- review scope.
- findings.
- required corrections or approval notes.
- explicit statement that publication/release remains blocked unless all downstream gates pass.

## Publication Boundary

Native-language review, when performed, is a language-quality gate only. It does not by itself approve technical accuracy, final sync/proof, metadata, ISBN, proof, publication readiness, release approval, or upload readiness.

## Authority Boundary

Book 1 remains the canonical English owner for manuscript text, claims, citations, permissions, and readiness. Multilingual Production owns Spanish native-review routing and workflow records.
